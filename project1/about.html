about t-Max jlateh jr

I am a Liberian native from West Africa. I grew up in the city-center, Monrovia, in a middle-class neighborhood called New Matadi Estate. Like every other regular kid, my childhood was a fun one. I had siblings and friends around to play with and we made a lot of good memories together.

Growing up, I always had a passion for art but didn't how I was going to turn that passion into a career. In high school, I did Arts, Science, and Business related subjects because my school didn't allow majoring. I was exposed to a lot of information with less depth into any of them. It was difficult for me to figure out what I wanted to do because I was pretty good at all of those subjects. I was in a state of a dilemma until 2016 when I graduated.

After I graduated high school, I had a sudden love for coding but again, I didn't know what to do within the Technology space because everything was new to me. I didn't have any foundational level education but I still chose to go ahead and do something that was Technology-related for my undergrad. I did my research and decided to pursue a Bachelor's in Information Technology. I did my undergrad at Bluecrest University College, Ghana, West Africa. The experience was a good one but the structure of the program was similar to that of my high school curriculum. From the first to the last semester, we were assigned courses automatically. We didn't have the choice of selecting electives. The school chose electives in Procurement, Accounting, Management, and Entrepreneurship. Again, a lot of information with no depth. I graduated college with a Summa Cum Laude but still didn't know what my career path was.

Upon graduation, I decided to take courses on Coursera, EdX, and attend a Full-stack Web Development Bootcamp whilst I awaited RIT admission confirmation. It was during that time I believe that I found myself and figured out what I want to do for the next 10-15 years. To this day, I still have that burning desire of channelling my ideas and innovation through graphics, web design, and development.
